Overview

The gap between brands and creators shouldn't need a middleman DM.

KnotxmeMe exists because the two sides that need each other most — brands with a budget and no reach, creators with reach and no brief — spend most of their time just trying to find one another.

The problem

Discovery is broken on both sides

Brands scroll Instagram hashtags and DM creators cold, with no idea if they've collaborated with a competitor last month or ghosted three brands before. Creators pitch brands blind, chase replies, and have no way to prove their track record beyond screenshots.

Neither side lacks ambition. They lack a shared, honest place to find each other.

The name

Knot. X. Me.

A knot ties two separate threads into one, stronger than either alone. That's the whole model: knot a brand to a creator — knot them to me — and let the collaboration hold on its own after that.

What we're building

One platform, three jobs

Discovery, outreach and proof — usually scattered across a spreadsheet, an inbox and a highlights reel — brought under one roof.

  • 01

    A filtered directory, not a feed

    Brands search creators by niche, platform, audience size, engagement rate, location and budget range. Creators search brands the same way — by category, past campaign type, and typical collaboration budget.

  • 02

    Outreach that's automated but not spammy

    Once a shortlist is built, Knotxme sends targeted, tracked emails on your behalf — hundreds of relevant options reached out to at once, without either side manually chasing every contact.

  • 03

    Profiles that carry a track record

    Every profile shows who a creator has actually collaborated with, and which brands are actively working with creators right now — so trust is visible before the first message is even sent.

  • 04

    A feedback layer that stays honest

    Collaborations that happen through Knotxme can be reviewed by both sides. Real outcomes, shown next to real feedback — not curated screenshots.

Why filters, not a feed

Because "popular" and "right fit" are different things

A feed rewards whoever's loudest. Filters reward whoever actually matches your brief — the same logic runs in reverse for creators searching for brands worth pitching.

More filters are planned as the platform grows: language, location, past brand categories, and typical collaboration budget.

02 · Outreach

Hundreds of intros, sent without hundreds of hours

Once a shortlist is built, Knotxme sends targeted, automated emails to every match — a brand's brief to a hundred fitting creators, or a creator's profile to every brand that's a genuine fit.

Every message is tracked, so you can see who opened, who replied, and who's worth following up with — instead of guessing.

Our standard

Three rules for the results board

  • 01

    Only collaborations made through Knotxme get reviewed

    No importing outside brand deals or inflating a profile with unrelated work.

  • 02

    Both sides get to speak

    A one-sided review isn't proof of anything. Brand and creator feedback sit side by side.

  • 03

    Feedback isn't editable after the fact

    Once submitted, a review reflects what happened — not what either side wishes had happened.
